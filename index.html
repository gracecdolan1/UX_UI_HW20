SALIDA MOUNTAIN TRAILS WEBSITE REDESIGN

Salida Mountain Trails (SMT) is a volunteer-run organization committed to building sustainable, non-motorized, multi-user trails near Salida, CO. The goal of this project was to redesign their website to help elevate their mission and make it easier for users to get involved.
ROLE
User Research, Interaction, Visual Design, Prototyping & Testing

PROCESS

To accomplish the redesign, the key phases of the design thinking process were utilized: Using the key phases of the design thinking process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test.

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

To begin our user research to understand the problem better, a Google survey targeted toward trail users and mountain bikers was distributed. 3 key pieces of information were found. 1. Trail users turn to social media (preferably instragram) to develop a connection to non-profits. 2. Trail users want to give back but often do not because they feel disconnected or intimidated. 3. Salida is a well know mountain biking destination in Colorado.

1:1 user interviews were also conducted with questions targeted toward understanding how users interact with nonprofits.
